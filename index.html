Importance of Emergency Hotlines

Emergency hotlines are your instant go-to when the need arises. That’s why it is important that you keep a copy of each of the important ones with you. Here are all the reasons why you should be keeping an emergency hotline with you at all times.

Climate change is real.

The effects of climate change have become very evident on Earth’s state. And it’s not of the good kind. The fast-rising shift of climate has had a negative impact on the inhabitants of this planet. Forest fires and drought spell disaster for any unprepared human being. You can call for help as soon as you sense something is going on if you have the emergency hotlines with you. You can even save the lives of those involved, even yourself.

Unexpected lifestyle diseases grow at a fast rate.

More and more people die of cardiovascular diseases such as heart failure, rheumatic heart disease and stroke due to the type of lifestyle that they maintain. That is one thing. What causes more concern is that most people don’t even know that they are at risk of having or that they have already acquired cardiovascular diseases. Strokes and other cardiovascular diseases can be triggered anytime, and the difference between saving a life and losing it matters on how long it takes to get the help. If you have an emergency number with you all the time, you can spare one’s life.

Accidents to human error and negligence are now becoming a huge thing.

A whopping number of 96,615 traffic accidents in the National Capital Region were recorded in 2015 according to MMDA. Furthermore, the same report also showed that most of these accidents were caused by human error. There’s just no stopping an accident when it’s bound to happen. Moreover, what one can do is be prepared by keeping a number to call when a collision happens just so help can arrive in the most immediate time.

Hotline Numbers

The need to keep a list of emergency numbers with you is a lifesaver. Most of all, if you care about your life, then you know what to do.

Here are the emergency hotlines that you need to bring with you at all times:

  1. National Emergency Hotline – 911 or 8888
  2. NDRRMC – 911-5061 to 65
  3. Red Cross – 143, (02) 527-0000, (02) 527-8385 to 95
  4. BFP – (02) 426-0219, (02) 426-3812, (02)426-0246
  5. PNP – 117 or 0917-847-5757

911 is the National Emergency Hotline

Whether you are planning for a trip to the Philippines or you are here already, there are numerous emergency numbers that you can depend on in case of an emergency. Here are some of the most commonly used numbers that you should know.

NATIONAL DISASTER RISK REDUCTION AND MANAGEMENT COUNCIL (NDRRMC)

The National Disaster Risk Reduction and Management Council (NDRRMC) is the lead agency responsible for the management and monitoring of the country’s disaster risk and risk reduction efforts.

Office of the Civil Defense Regional Office Telephone Directory

The Office of the Civil Defense Regional Office (OCDRO) is tasked to support local government units (LGUs) in disaster risk reduction (DRR) and disaster preparedness (DPR) programs. It is the main government agency responsible for provision of disaster risk reduction (DRR), disaster preparedness (DPR), disaster mitigation (DRM), disaster contingency (DRC), disaster risk reduction (DRR) and disaster response (DRR) services to LGUs in the country.

Department of Social Welfare and Development

The Department of Social Welfare and Development (DSWD) is an agency that works to help people who need help because of their social welfare needs. It is committed to providing quality social services to improve the quality of life of the people in the country.

RED CROSS HOTLINE

The Red Cross in the Philippines is a non-profit organization that provides aid and assistance to victims of natural and man-made disasters that have been declared by the Philippine government.
